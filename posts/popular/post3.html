How I Landed My Dream Role in Applied AI

From portfolio to final offer—here's the strategy that turned years of tinkering into a role building production-grade AI systems.

Lead with Impact Stories

Every conversation opened with a concise story: the problem, the model or system I built, and the measurable outcome. Numbers help hiring panels advocate for you.

Curate a Living Portfolio

I shipped case studies on my personal site showing code snippets, architecture diagrams, and Postman collections so reviewers could replicate the work.

Simulate the Interview Loop

  • Technical: Practiced pair programming and system design focused on data pipelines and evaluation.
  • Product: Prepared trade-off discussions: cost vs. latency, safety vs. coverage.
  • Leadership: Shared stories about mentoring, documentation, and cross-functional alignment.

Follow Up Thoughtfully

After each interview I sent a short recap: what I learned about the team, how I'd add value, and a resource they could use immediately.

If you want a role in applied AI, show that you can ship, measure, and communicate. The offers will follow.
