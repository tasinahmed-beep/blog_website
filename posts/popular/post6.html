Edge Computing for Real-Time AI Experiences

Edge inference is going mainstream. Understand when to adopt it, the trade-offs, and the stack that keeps latency low.

Why Push Inference to the Edge

Moving requests closer to users slashes network hops, reduces the blast radius of outages, and gives privacy-conscious customers peace of mind.

When Edge Makes Sense

  • Interactive assistants where turnaround time must be < 300 ms.
  • Use cases with strict data residency requirements.
  • IoT deployments where consistent connectivity is not guaranteed.

The Reference Stack

  1. Runtime: Cloudflare Workers or Fly.io Machines running quantized models.
  2. Storage: Durable Objects or LiteFS for low-latency state.
  3. Observability: Collect spans with OpenTelemetry and visualize per-region heatmaps.

Keep It Maintainable

Introduce feature flags to control rollout by geography, and always keep a central inference fallback for complex requests.

Edge is not the answer to everything, but for real-time AI experiences it turns impossibly slow into delightfully instant.
